CALL TRANSCRIPT
Deal: Pelham Bancorp
Call: 1 of 1 — Discovery with Manager of Cloud Operations
Date: 2026-07-21
Duration: 28 min
Attendees: Dana Whitfield (ShieldPoint, AE), Renee Alcott (Pelham Bancorp, Manager of Cloud Operations), Marcus Bell (Pelham Bancorp, Lead DevOps Engineer)

---

Dana: Renee, Marcus, thanks for making the time. You reached out through the website rather than the other way around, which is always interesting to me. What prompted it?

Renee: We had our exam in March. The examiners came back with a finding on cloud configuration governance. Not a formal enforcement action, but it's written up, and we have to show a remediation plan by the end of the year.

Dana: What did the finding actually say?

Renee: The wording was something like, insufficient evidence of continuous monitoring of cloud infrastructure configuration against an approved baseline. Which is fair. We do have a baseline document. We just can't prove anyone's checking against it.

Marcus: We check. We check manually, in a spreadsheet, once a quarter. That's the whole program.

Dana: How many accounts and subscriptions are you covering that way?

Marcus: Fourteen AWS accounts, four Azure subscriptions. Two of the AWS accounts came with an acquisition last year and I'd honestly call those partially mapped. I know what's in them. I couldn't swear to it in front of an examiner.

Dana: That's exactly the gap the platform closes — continuous evidence instead of a quarterly snapshot. Before we get into that, has anything actually gone wrong, or is this purely the exam finding?

Renee: Nothing that reached a customer. We had a database snapshot in a non-production account that was shared more broadly than it should have been. Marcus caught it during the manual review.

Marcus: Caught it eleven weeks after it was created, is the part I'd flag. If that had been production data we'd be having a very different conversation right now.

Dana: Do you have a sense of what the exam finding is costing you? Remediation effort, audit prep, anything quantified?

Renee: Not in dollars. I know it's eating a lot of Marcus's time and some of mine. Our internal audit group has picked up hours on it too. Nobody's added that up.

Marcus: There's no line item for it anywhere. It just comes out of whatever else we were going to do that quarter.

Dana: Understood. If you moved ahead with something like this, how does a purchase like that get approved at Pelham?

Renee: That's more complicated than it was six months ago. We just hired a CISO. She started three weeks ago.

Dana: Congratulations, that's usually a good thing for a project like this.

Renee: It should be. But she hasn't set her priorities yet, and there's an informal freeze on new security spend until she does. My director told me to keep doing discovery and be ready, but not to expect a signature before she's had time to look at the whole program.

Dana: Has she seen the exam finding?

Renee: I assume so. I haven't briefed her myself. I've met her once, in a group setting.

Dana: Would it be worth getting in front of her while she's still forming that picture? New security leaders usually want a short list of things that are already in motion.

Renee: Maybe. I don't want to be the person who walks in during week four with a vendor already picked out. That's a bad look and it's not really how things work here.

Dana: Fair enough. Marcus, on the technical side, what would this need to do on day one?

Marcus: Honestly? Tell me what's broken across all eighteen environments without me writing another script. Everything after that is a bonus.

Dana: Any container footprint?

Marcus: ECS mostly, some EKS in the newer accounts. And we're moving more that direction, so whatever we pick needs to keep up.

Dana: Have you written down what you need it to do? A requirements list, evaluation criteria, anything the team has agreed on?

Renee: No. We've got the exam finding, which tells us the outcome we need. We haven't translated that into what a tool has to do.

Dana: Is that something you'd want help structuring?

Renee: Possibly, but I'd want it to be ours, not a vendor's. No offense.

Dana: None taken, that's the right instinct. Are you talking to anyone else?

Renee: We've had one conversation with Aegis Cloud. Our internal audit director used to work somewhere that ran their product, so she brought them in.

Dana: How did that go?

Marcus: Their compliance reporting is strong. That's what audit cares about. The engineering side felt thinner to me but I only saw a demo, so take that for what it's worth.

Renee: And that's kind of the problem. Audit likes one thing, engineering likes another, and there's no agreed way to settle it. Whoever tells the best story to the CISO probably wins, and I don't love that.

Dana: I'd rather win on criteria than on storytelling, for what it's worth. What would be a useful next step that doesn't require anybody's signature?

Marcus: Point it at one of the acquisition accounts. The messy ones. If it finds things I don't already know about, that tells me more than any demo would.

Dana: We can do that read-only, scoped to one account, and you'd have findings the same day. No commitment attached.

Renee: I'm fine with that as long as it's non-production and we tell the account owner first.

Dana: I'll send scoping steps this afternoon. Anything else before we wrap?

Renee: No, let's see what it turns up.

Dana: Thanks both.

Marcus: Thanks.

--- END OF CALL ---
